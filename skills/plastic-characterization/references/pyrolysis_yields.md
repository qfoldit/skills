# Literature ranges for pyrolysis product yield by polymer type

Below are generalized ranges, widely cited in the open plastic-pyrolysis literature (typical thermal pyrolysis, 450-550°C, no catalyst unless otherwise noted). These are approximate figures for first-pass estimation, not precise constants — actual yield depends on the specific installation, heating rate, residence time, and presence of a catalyst.

| Polymer | Oil (liquid fraction), wt.% | Gas, wt.% | Solid residue/char, wt.% | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PE (polyethylene) | 70-85 | 10-25 | 1-5 | Gives the most "fuel-like" oil (paraffins/olefins), low char content |
| PP (polypropylene) | 70-80 | 15-25 | 1-5 | Similar to PE, slightly higher gas fraction |
| PS (polystyrene) | 60-80 | 5-15 | 5-15 | Oil is aromatic-rich (styrene, toluene), may need additional refining |
| PET (polyethylene terephthalate) | 20-40 | 15-30 | 30-40 (including terephthalic acid/solid products) | Produces a lot of solid/acidic products, not a clean "liquid fuel" |
| PVC (polyvinyl chloride) | 30-45 (after degradation) | 20-30 | 15-25 | **Key risk:** releases HCl/organic chlorine — at high fractions in the mix, contaminates the oil with chlorine and damages equipment (corrosion); requires neutralization or upstream dechlorination |
| Other/mixed | 40-60 (wide spread) | 15-30 | 10-30 | Conservative averaged estimate in the absence of detailed composition data |

## Chlorine from PVC — rough estimate

Rough rule of thumb: PVC fraction in the mix (wt.%) × ~0.5-0.7 ≈ expected fraction of the original chlorine that will end up in the oil without an upstream dechlorination step (two-stage pyrolysis with HCl removal in the first stage significantly reduces this figure). Even 3-5% PVC in the mix can render the oil unsuitable for many applications without further refining.

## Sources (for understanding where the ranges come from)

The ranges are generalized from results repeatedly reproduced across independent laboratory studies of thermal pyrolysis of pure household polymers (not a single specific paper) — for that reason, no single precise citation is given here. Before using these figures in investor- or partner-facing materials, it is recommended to:
1. Run at least 5-10 of your own laboratory tests on real feedstock.
2. Compare against these reference ranges as a starting point, not a final result.
